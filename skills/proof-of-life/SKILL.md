# Proof Of Life

Separate four questions:

- Is the person alive?
- Are they physically and cognitively capable?
- Did they personally appear or speak?
- Could statements be produced by an office, family, aide, or institution?

## Workflow

1. Search for dated video/audio first.
2. Search for current photos with independent time/place markers.
3. Treat written statements, signatures, and secondhand claims as weak proof-of-life.
4. Use image/video providers only when there is actual media to test.
5. Compare death/incapacity rumors against official and independent sources.
6. Register evidence for and against each hypothesis.

## Evidence Strength

- Strong: current live video/audio with verifiable recent context.
- Medium: credible official or foreign-government claim of recent direct engagement.
- Weak: signed text, read-out statement, office-hosted event, portrait, mural, archival footage.
